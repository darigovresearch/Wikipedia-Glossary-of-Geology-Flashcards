<dd class=""glossary"">The geologic <a href=""#epoch""><span title=""See entry on this page at § epoch"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">epoch</span></a> which lasted from about 2,588,000 to 11,700 years ago, spanning the world's recent period of repeated glaciations. The Pleistocene is the first epoch of the <a href=""#quaternary""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Quaternary"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Quaternary Period</span></a> and the sixth epoch of the <a href=""#cenozoic_Era""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Cenozoic Era"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Cenozoic Era</span></a>.</dd>

"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pliocene"" title=""Pliocene"">Pliocene</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">The geologic <a href=""#period""><span title=""See entry on this page at § period"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">period</span></a> that extends from 5.332 million to 2.588[2] million years Before Present. It is the second-youngest epoch of the <a href=""#neogene""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Neogene"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Neogene Period</span></a> in the <a href=""#cenozoic_Era""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Cenozoic Era"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Cenozoic Era</span></a>. The Pliocene follows the <a href=""#miocene""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Miocene"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Miocene</span></a> and is followed by the <a href=""#pleistocene""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Pleistocene"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Pleistocene</span></a>.</dd>
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plutonic"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Plutonic"">plutonic</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">Having crystallised at depth within the Earth's <a href=""#crust""><span title=""See entry on this page at § crust"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">crust</span></a>, used of a rock. Plutonic rocks are slow-cooling and coarse-grained and have relatively low temperatures of final consolidation.</dd>

"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psammite"" title=""Psammite"">psammite</a>"|"

"

"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pumice"" title=""Pumice"">pumice</a>"|"

"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyroclastic_flow"" title=""Pyroclastic flow"">pyroclastic flow</a>"|"

"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyroclastic"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Pyroclastic"">pyroclastic</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">A volcanic fragment, such as a volcanic bomb, <a href=""#breadcrust_bomb""><span title=""See entry on this page at § breadcrust bomb"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">breadcrust bomb</span></a>, or <a href=""#achnelith""><span title=""See entry on this page at § achnelith"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">achnelith</span></a>.</dd>
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyroxene"" title=""Pyroxene"">pyroxene</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">Any of a set of mineral species which, although falling into different systems (orthorhombic, monoclinic, and triclinic), are closely related in form and structure. They are metasilicates of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcium"" title=""Calcium"">calcium</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium"" title=""Magnesium"">magnesium</a>, and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron"" title=""Iron"">iron</a> with <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manganese"" title=""Manganese"">manganese</a>, and less often with <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium"" title=""Sodium"">sodium</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potassium"" title=""Potassium"">potassium</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zirconium"" title=""Zirconium"">zirconium</a>, and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluorine"" title=""Fluorine"">fluorine</a>.</dd>
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyroxenite"" title=""Pyroxenite"">pyroxenite</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">A coarse-grained, <a href=""#holocrystalline""><span title=""See entry on this page at § holocrystalline"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">holocrystalline</span></a> igneous rock consisting mainly of <a href=""#pyroxene""><span title=""See entry on this page at § pyroxene"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">pyroxenes</span></a>. It may contain <a href=""#biotite""><span title=""See entry on this page at § biotite"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">biotite</span></a>, <a href=""#hornblende""><span title=""See entry on this page at § hornblende"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">hornblende</span></a>, or <a href=""#olivine""><span title=""See entry on this page at § olivine"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">olivine</span></a> as accessories.</dd>
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quartzite"" title=""Quartzite"">quartzite</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">A compact, hard, very fine-grained white to creamy white rock which breaks into sharp angular fragments. Quartzite is always associated with other <a href=""#metamorphic_rock""><span title=""See entry on this page at § metamorphic rock"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">metamorphic rocks</span></a>, while cemented sandstone is always associated with other <a href=""#sedimentary_rock""><span title=""See entry on this page at § sedimentary rock"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">sedimentary rocks</span></a>.</dd>
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quaternary"" title=""Quaternary"">Quaternary</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">The most recent of the three <a href=""#period""><span title=""See entry on this page at § period"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">periods</span></a> of the <a href=""#cenozoic_Era""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Cenozoic Era"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Cenozoic Era</span></a> in the standard geologic time scale. It follows the Neogene Period, spanning 2.588 ± 0.005 million years ago to the present.</dd>
"

"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retrograde_metamorphism"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Retrograde metamorphism"">retrograde metamorphism</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">The reconstitution of a rock via revolatisation under decreasing temperatures (and usually pressures), allowing the mineral assemblages formed in prograde metamorphism to revert to those more stable at less extreme conditions.</dd>
